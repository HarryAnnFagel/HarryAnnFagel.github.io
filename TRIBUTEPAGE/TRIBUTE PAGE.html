Michael Jordan

The legend of basketball

Here's a time line of Michael Jordan's life:

  • 1963 - Born in Brooklyn, New York
  • 1978 - Begins his high school basketball career at Emsley A. Laney High School in Wilmington, North Carolina
  • 1981 - Earns a basketball scholarship to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill
  • 1982 - Hits the game-winning shot to win the NCAA Championship for UNC
  • 1984 - Drafted by the Chicago Bulls as the third overall pick in the NBA Draft
  • 1985 - Named NBA Rookie of the Year
  • 1986 - Scores 63 points in a playoff game against the Boston Celtics, a record that still stands today
  • 1991 - Leads the Bulls to their first NBA Championship, winning the Finals MVP award
  • 1992 - Wins his second NBA Championship and also wins a gold medal with the Dream Team at the Barcelona Olympics
  • 1993 - Retires from basketball for the first time to pursue a career in baseball
  • 1995 - Returns to the NBA and rejoins the Chicago Bulls
  • 1996 - Wins his fourth NBA Championship and an Olympic gold medal at the Atlanta Olympics
  • 1998 - Wins his sixth NBA Championship with the Bulls, earning his sixth Finals MVP award
  • 2001 - Retires from basketball for the second time
  • 2001 - Becomes part-owner and executive for the Washington Wizards
  • 2009 - Inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame
  • 2020 - Releases "The Last Dance," a documentary series chronicling his career with the Chicago Bulls

"Jordan's influence on the game of basketball and culture in general is immeasurable. His skill, drive, and competitiveness set the standard for greatness."

-- NBA.com
